MY SKILLS

USER RESEARCH

I begin this process by defining the research objective, identifying user groups, and creating a potential user, or proto-persona. I collect both qualitative and quantitative data through a variety of methods. User interviews are conducted to collect qualitative data and observe how users interact with the product at hand. I also administer questionnaires, scour customer reviews, and compare competitors’ products. I then compile my findings into conclusions that ensure all solutions best address both user and stakeholder needs and remain rooted in user research.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

A critical outcome of thorough user research is insight into how users navigate through and use a product. Mapping out these various user paths paints a large picture of where exactly the issues lay. With this knowledge at hand, I can reorganize the navigation design and content organization for increased functionality and seamless navigation.

PROTOTYPING & TESTING

Prototyping is where my research, ideas, understanding, and proposed solutions are put into practice. Sketching wireframes on paper helps me think through each design decision as I continually refer back to my user research. Figma is where I build out the low-fidelity wireframes which focus not on design, but on the product’s navigation and content structure.
